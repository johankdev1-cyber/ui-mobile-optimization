The audit

6 things that were broken

No mobile layout
The desktop grid was shrinking down instead of reflowing. Three-column grids became illegible at 375px.
→ Full mobile-first rebuild with 2-column max
No search on mobile
The search bar was hidden behind a toggle that was 8px tall. Untappable on any real device.
→ Persistent, tap-friendly search in the nav
Slow load (6.4s)
Uncompressed images, 14 font weights loaded, render-blocking scripts. Painful on mobile data.
→ Down to 2.1s with lazy loading + cleanup
Tiny tap targets
Add to cart buttons were 28px tall. Apple guidelines require 44px minimum. Users were mis-tapping constantly.
→ All interactive elements at 44px+ height
Broken checkout
The address form used fixed widths that overflowed on mobile. The payment step was completely broken on iOS Safari.
→ Rebuilt with fluid inputs and iOS keyboard handling
No hierarchy on mobile
Every element felt the same size. No clear path for the eye to follow. Users didn't know where to look or tap first.
→ Clear type scale, white space, and visual hierarchy
Results

What changed after launch

Within 30 days of the redesigned mobile experience going live, the client saw a dramatic shift in their mobile metrics. What was previously their worst-performing channel became their best.

The improvements weren't from adding features — they came from removing friction and respecting how people actually use their phones.
